What Is ODI Quant?
Your edge before the market opens

ODI Quant is a pre-market research tool for NSE equity traders. It fetches end-of-day (EOD) data every evening, runs technical analysis on 100+ stocks, and tells you exactly which setups to watch the next morning — with entry prices, stop-losses, and targets already calculated.

Think of it as your trading desk doing all the homework overnight. You arrive at 9:00 AM with a prepared watchlist instead of scrambling through charts.

💡
Core philosophy: Signals are generated from yesterday's closing data. All entry levels, stops, and targets are reference points — you must confirm them live at market open before executing. Never pre-place orders blindly based on EOD signals.
Recommended Daily Workflow
Previous Evening (After 4 PM)
Run the pipeline — Click "Run Analysis" on the Stock Scanner page. Takes 60–90 seconds. All pages refresh with fresh data.
8:45 – 9:00 AM
Check Global Sentiment — Open Stock Scanner. Look at the top bar: S&P 500, NASDAQ, Nifty direction. STRONG_BULLISH = green light for longs. STRONG_BEARISH = tighten up.
9:00 – 9:15 AM (Pre-market)
Review your watchlists — Check IntraContra for intraday setups (PDH/PDL alerts). Check Sniper for swing trade patterns. Note your entry triggers and gap invalidation levels.
9:15 – 9:30 AM (Market Open)
Observe only — Never trade the first 15 minutes. Watch if your setups are gapping up/down. If a stock opens more than 1% above your entry trigger, the setup is invalid — skip it.
9:30 AM onwards
Execute confirmed setups — Only enter if the stock confirms your trigger with volume. Use the position sizes shown. Respect your stop-loss. Exit T1 target at 40%, trail the rest.
3:15 PM
Close intraday positions — All IntraContra (intraday) trades must be flat by 3:15 PM. Swing trades (Sniper) carry overnight as intended.
🛑 Daily Stop Rule
If you lose more than 2% of capital in a single day, close the screen and stop trading. No exceptions. Losses compound — revenge trading makes it worse.
⚠️ Max Positions Rule
Never hold more than 5 open positions at once. All signals on a single day are correlated — if the market falls, they all fall together.
📐 Sector Limit Rule
No more than 25% of capital in any one sector at a time. Three banking stocks all long = concentrated bet on one sector.

Page 1: Stock Scanner
Your daily signal engine — the starting point for every trading day
STRATEGY: Multi-Factor Technical Screening | UNIVERSE: 100+ NSE stocks | TIMEFRAME: Swing (1–14 days)

The Stock Scanner is the heart of ODI Quant. Every evening it analyzes over 100 NSE stocks using five independent technical signals and assigns each stock a score from 0 to 95. Stocks scoring above 70 are classified as High Probability setups — your best trade candidates for the next session.

How the Scoring Works

Each stock is scored across five dimensions. Think of it like five independent judges — the more that agree, the stronger the signal:

Trend Score 25 pts
Are the moving averages (MA20, MA50, MA200) lined up in the right direction? Price above all three MAs = full score. The trend must be established before entering.
Breakout Score 25 pts
Is the stock breaking out above its 52-week high (not just a 20-day high)? A 52-week breakout means the stock has cleared months of overhead resistance — institutions take notice.
Volume Score 20 pts
Is volume today meaningfully higher than average? AND is the stock's volume spike bigger than its sector's spike? This filters out market-wide F&O expiry days where everything looks like it's moving on volume.
Volatility Score 15 pts
Is the Average True Range (ATR) expanding? ATR expansion means the stock's daily range is growing — momentum is building. Contracting ATR = the move is fizzling.
RSI Score 10 pts
Is RSI between 40–65? This is the "sweet spot" — the stock has momentum but isn't overbought yet. RSI above 70 = signal is suppressed (too extended). RSI below 30 = signal is suppressed (too beaten down).
Reading the Signal Categories
HIGH_PROB_LONG
Score ≥ 70. All major signals align bullish. This is your priority watchlist for long entries. Expect 3–5 of these per scan day on average.
HIGH_PROB_SHORT
Score ≥ 70, bearish direction. All signals align for a downside move. Only trade short setups when Global Sentiment is BEARISH or NEUTRAL — never short into a strong bull market.
WATCHLIST
Score 50–69. Setup is building but not fully confirmed. Add to your watchlist and check again next day. These often become HIGH_PROB on the following session.
NO_TRADE
Score below 50. No edge present. Do not trade. The system shows these for transparency so you understand why a stock was rejected.

Page 2: NIFTY Options
Next-day NIFTY 50 options strategy with Black-Scholes pricing
STRATEGY: Directional Options | INSTRUMENT: NIFTY 50 Index Options | TIMEFRAME: Next-day (1 DTE)

This page analyses NIFTY 50's technical position and recommends a specific options strategy for the next trading session. Options are derivative instruments — you pay a small premium upfront to control a large notional position. The reward can be 3–10× the premium paid, but you can lose 100% of the premium if wrong.

🚨
Options Warning: Options are high-risk instruments. Never risk more than 1–2% of capital on a single options trade. The premium shown is an estimate using Black-Scholes — always verify actual premiums on NSE before trading. Options can expire worthless.
Greeks — What They Mean for You

The "Greeks" are numbers that describe how an option's price will move. You don't need to calculate them — the system does it. You just need to know what they're telling you:

Δ
Delta
How much the option price moves for every ₹1 move in NIFTY. Delta of 0.5 means if NIFTY moves ₹100, your option gains ₹50. Higher delta = more responsive to the index move.
Θ
Theta (Time Decay)
How much value the option loses every day just by time passing. Theta of -₹50 means your option loses ₹50 in value overnight even if NIFTY doesn't move. This is the options buyer's enemy.
γ
Gamma
How fast Delta itself changes. High gamma near expiry means small NIFTY moves create large delta swings — the option becomes very sensitive. Good for quick moves, risky if wrong.
IV
Implied Volatility
The market's expectation of future movement, expressed as annualized %. High IV = expensive options (you're paying a premium for uncertainty). Low IV = cheap options. The system uses 30-day Historical Volatility as a proxy.
How to Use This Page
1
Click "Analyse NIFTY" to fetch fresh data. The system looks at NIFTY's trend (MA20/50/200), ATR, RSI, VIX, and global sentiment.
2

IV Environments Explained
LOW IV
Options are cheap. Buying premium is attractive. Good time for directional bets (buy calls or puts).
MODERATE IV
Normal environment. Standard strategies work. Don't overpay — size moderately.
ELEVATED IV
Options are expensive. Avoid buying premium — you'll pay too much for the right to profit. Selling spreads is better here.

Page 3: Sniper (Trend-Pullback)
Find the best swing trade setups in NIFTY 100 stocks
STRATEGY: Trend Following + Pullback Entry | UNIVERSE: NIFTY 100 | TIMEFRAME: Swing (1–4 weeks)

The Sniper page uses a professional 7-phase framework to find stocks in strong uptrends that have pulled back to a key support level — ready to resume their move. This is how institutional momentum traders operate: ride the trend, but enter on the pullback (not at the top).

The logic: a stock that has proven itself by reaching a 52-week high, then pulls back to its 21-day EMA with contracting volume, is a high-probability buy. Volume contraction during a pullback signals that sellers are weak — smart money is holding. When volume expands again, the move resumes.

The 7 Phases — Step by Step
Phase 1 Market Context Filter

Before looking at any individual stock, the system checks whether the overall market environment supports buying. If the market itself is broken, no individual stock setup matters.

  • Nifty 50 vs 50 EMA: If Nifty is below its 50-day EMA, the system switches to CAUTION mode. Don't aggressively buy new positions.
  • India VIX: The "fear gauge." Below 18 = calm, deploy 100% capital. 18–22 = caution, deploy 70%. Above 22 = high fear, stay in cash. You cannot fight a panicking market.
💡
VIX above 22 is not a failure — it's the system protecting your capital. The best trades happen when VIX drops back below 18 and the market resumes its uptrend.
Phase 2 Sector Rotation

Not all sectors move at the same time. The system ranks 10 NSE sectoral indices by their 4-week relative strength vs Nifty. You want to buy stocks in the sectors that are leading, not lagging.

The top 3 ranked sectors (marked 🔥) are where the best stocks are likely hiding. A banking stock is more likely to succeed when the banking sector ETF is outperforming. Sector rotation is how institutional money flows — follow it.

  • 4-Week RS: How much stronger/weaker this sector has been vs Nifty over the past month.
  • 1-Week Momentum: ACCELERATING means the sector is picking up speed. DECELERATING means it's peaking — be cautious.
Phase 3 Quality Gate

Every NIFTY 100 stock passes through two mandatory filters before being analyzed:

  • Close > 200 EMA: The stock must be in a long-term uptrend. Never buy a stock that's below its 200-day average — that's a downtrend.
  • Daily Turnover ≥ ₹10 Crore: The stock must be liquid enough to enter and exit cleanly. Illiquid stocks produce bad fills and wide spreads.

Stocks within 10% of their 52-week high are ranked first — these are breakout candidates with proven institutional interest.

Phase 4 Pattern Arsenal — The 3 Setups

After passing quality gates, each stock is checked for three specific patterns. These are the actual trade setups:

📐 EMA Pullback
What it is: The stock is in an uptrend (above 50 EMA) and has pulled back close to its 21-day EMA (within 3%). Volume contracted during the pullback (sellers dried up), then started picking up again — signaling resumption.
Entry: Current price | Stop: 21 EMA × 0.99 | Targets: 2×ATR, 3×ATR, 4×ATR
Why it works: The 21 EMA acts as a magnet for institutional buy programs. When a stock that was going up comes back to test this level on low volume, big funds are buying quietly. The volume pickup confirms they're done accumulating and ready to push higher.
🚀 Base Breakout (VCP)
What it is: The stock spent 4–8 weeks in a tight consolidation range (less than 8% total range), then broke above with 2× average volume and a strong close (above 60% of the day's range). This is Mark Minervini's Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP).
Entry: Break above 30-day high | Stop: Base high × 0.98 | Targets: 2×ATR, 4×ATR, 6×ATR
Why it works: A tight base means institutional investors have been absorbing supply (buying from weak hands) without letting the price fall. When they're done accumulating, volume explodes upward. This is how stocks go from ₹500 to ₹1,000 in a year.
⬆️ Gap Reversal
What it is: The stock gapped up/down by 1.5%+ on a previous day, then consolidated tightly within the gap-day's range (± 1.2%). When price breaks back above the gap-day's high, the reversal is confirmed.
Entry: Gap high + 0.1% | Stop: Consolidation low | Targets: 2×ATR, 3×ATR, 4×ATR
Why it works: A gap-up on strong earnings or news creates a new "floor" of buyers who entered on the gap. If the stock consolidates in a tight range (rather than immediately selling off), it shows conviction. The subsequent breakout is clean — everyone who didn't buy on the gap now chases.
Phases 5–7 Position Sizing, Trade Management & Review

Use the Position Calculator built into the page. Enter your total capital, the entry price, and your stop-loss — the calculator tells you exactly how many shares to buy so you never risk more than your chosen percentage.

  • At T1 (1:2 R:R): Exit 50% of position. Move stop-loss to your entry price (breakeven). Now you cannot lose money on this trade.
  • Remaining 50%: Trail with the 10-day EMA. Sell only if price closes below the 10 EMA on the daily chart.
  • Max 6 open Sniper positions at once (Portfolio heat ≤ 12% of capital).
  • Monthly drawdown limit: If down 8% in a month, stop all new Sniper trades for the rest of the month.

Page 4: IntraContra
Intraday level-based setups on 20 high-liquidity F&O stocks
STRATEGY: PDH/PDL Level Breakouts + Session TP Mean Reversion | UNIVERSE: 20 curated F&O names | TIMEFRAME: Intraday (same day)

IntraContra is a pre-market preparation tool for intraday traders. It analyzes 20 of India's most liquid Nifty stocks every evening and tells you exactly which levels to watch the next morning, what setups to expect, and how to size your positions.

⚠️
Important: IntraContra generates EOD-based alerts — not live signals. The setups shown are levels to watch at market open. You must confirm with live price action and volume before entering. These are pre-market homework assignments, not automated buy/sell orders.
Key Concepts You Must Know
PDH / PDL
Previous Day High / Previous Day Low. The most important intraday reference levels. Institutional algorithms are programmed to defend these levels. A clean break above PDH with volume = bullish. A break below PDL with volume = bearish. These levels are where the most reliable intraday moves happen.
Session TP (Typical Price)
(PDH + PDL + Close) ÷ 3. This is the prior session's "fair value" — the average price around which the previous day's volume was concentrated. When price deviates more than 1.5% from this level early in the day, there's a statistical tendency to revert back toward it. This is your mean-reversion anchor.
20D Rolling VWAP
Volume-Weighted Average Price over 20 days. This tells you the swing trend: above 20D VWAP = bullish multi-day regime, below = bearish. Used only for directional bias — not as an intraday entry level (it moves too slowly for that).
ATR (Average True Range)
The stock's average daily move over 14 days. A stock with 1.5% ATR typically moves ₹15 on a ₹1,000 stock per day. For qualification, the system requires ATR ≥ 1.5% — if the stock doesn't move enough, there's no opportunity to profit intraday.
The 5 Intraday Setups
🟢 PDH Breakout (Bullish Continuation)
Trigger: Yesterday's close was within 0–2% above PDH. Swing bias is bullish (above 20D VWAP). 9 EMA aligned upward.
Action: Watch for price to break and hold above PDH at open with above-average volume. Entry just above PDH (PDH × 1.002). Stop-loss 0.5% below PDH. Targets: 2×ATR and 3×ATR.
Why it works: PDH acts as a magnet. Traders who bought yesterday at the high are break-even — any move above is profit for them, and new buyers push the momentum. Institutional algorithms run at PDH levels.
🔴 PDL Breakdown (Bearish Continuation)
Trigger: Yesterday's close was within 0–2% below PDL. Swing bias is bearish (below 20D VWAP). 9 EMA pointing down.
Action: Watch for price to break and hold below PDL at open with volume. Short entry just below PDL. Stop-loss 0.5% above PDL.
Why it works: Mirror of PDH. Yesterday's low is where sellers took control. Breaking below with volume signals that sellers are dominating and the move continues down.
↩️ Session TP Reversion — Long (Mean Reversion)
Trigger: Price is 1.5%+ BELOW Session TP AND RSI is below 38 (oversold). Only valid 9:30 AM – 11:30 AM.
Action: Buy at current price. Stop-loss below 3-day swing low. Target is the Session TP level (your mean-reversion anchor).
Why it works: In the early session, price tends to mean-revert toward the prior day's fair value. If the stock has been beaten down 1.5%+ from that anchor and RSI shows capitulation, a bounce back toward the anchor is highly probable. The window closes by 11:30 AM — after that, the market has found a new direction.
↪️ Session TP Reversion — Short (Mean Reversion)
Trigger: Price is 1.5%+ ABOVE Session TP AND RSI is above 62. Only valid 9:30 AM – 11:30 AM.
Action: Short at current price. Stop-loss above 3-day swing high. Target is the Session TP level.
Why it works: Same principle as above, reversed. Morning euphoria often overshoots the prior day's anchor. When RSI confirms overextension, the reversion is reliable within the morning window.
⬆️⬇️ Gap Plays
Trigger: Stock gapped up/down by 0.8%+ from previous close. First 15 minutes: observe only.
Action: After 9:30 AM, if the gap is holding (stock stays above gap-open), trade in the gap direction. If gap starts filling (stock reverting to previous close), fade it.
Why it works: Gaps create reference levels. Gaps that hold (don't fill) attract momentum traders. Gaps that fail (fill back) attract mean-reversion traders. Both are tradeable — you just need to wait to see which scenario unfolds.
Trading Time Windows — Critical

The time of day matters enormously in intraday trading. Each window has different market dynamics:

9:00 – 9:15 AM
Pre-Market Prep
Review IntraContra watchlist. Note your levels. Set price alerts. No orders yet.
9:15 – 9:30 AM
Opening Chaos
Observe only. Never trade the first 15 minutes — liquidity is thin and direction is unpredictable. Watch which direction the market opens.
9:30 – 10:15 AM
Prime Window
The best time for PDH/PDL breakouts and gap plays. Volume is highest. Moves are cleanest. This is when you execute.
10:15 – 11:30 AM
Momentum + Reversion
Continue to hold winning trades from the prime window. Good time for Session TP reversion setups. Trend continuation trades still work.
11:30 AM – 12:30 PM
DEAD ZONE — No Trades
Lunch hour. Volume collapses. Random choppy moves. This is when bad trades happen. Put down the screen.
12:30 – 2:00 PM
Afternoon Session
Institutional activity resumes. Continuation of morning trends or new direction. Be cautious — assess risk vs. time remaining.
2:00 – 3:15 PM
Exit Window

Page 5: BigBag
Long-term compounders screened by the EMPIRE quality framework
STRATEGY: Quality-at-Reasonable-Price Compounding | UNIVERSE: 47 curated quality stocks | TIMEFRAME: 1–10 years

BigBag is not a trading strategy — it's an investing strategy. The goal is to identify businesses that can grow their earnings at 20%+ per year for a decade, buy them at reasonable valuations, and hold through volatility. This is how ₹5 lakh becomes ₹50 lakh over 7–10 years without constant trading.

The philosophy: instead of making 100 small trades, find 5–10 exceptional businesses and let compounding do the heavy lifting. Warren Buffett calls this "buying wonderful companies at fair prices."

The EMPIRE Framework — 6 Quality Filters

Every stock in the universe is scored across 6 dimensions. Each has a maximum score — stocks are ranked by their total EMPIRE score (0–100):

E
Earnings Quality 20 pts
Return on Equity (ROE) ≥ 25% = 20 pts. ROE measures how efficiently the business uses shareholder money to generate profit. A 25% ROE means for every ₹100 of equity, the business earns ₹25 in profit — exceptional. Below 15% = 0 pts.
M
EPS Momentum 20 pts
EPS (Earnings Per Share) growth rate. ≥ 25% annual growth = 20 pts. This is the most important single number for stock price appreciation. A company growing earnings at 25% per year will, on average, see its stock price double every 3 years (if valuation holds).
P
Profit Margin 15 pts
Operating margin ≥ 20% = 15 pts. High margins = pricing power = moat. A business that can charge more than competitors without losing customers is protected from disruption. Low margins mean the business is a commodity — vulnerable to competition and cost pressures.
I
Revenue Growth 15 pts
Top-line revenue growth ≥ 20% = 15 pts. Companies can sometimes inflate EPS by cutting costs without growing revenue — the P score catches this. You want revenue and earnings growing together. Revenue growth without earnings growth = bad management. Earnings growth without revenue growth = unsustainable.
R
Resilience (Debt) 15 pts
Debt-to-Equity ratio ≤ 0.1 = 15 pts. Low debt companies survive downturns. High debt companies get destroyed in recessions — they may be forced to dilute equity or default. A D/E above 0.5 = 0 pts. Exception: banking and finance companies are excluded from this filter (leverage is their business model).
E
Entry Valuation (PEG) 15 pts
PEG ratio < 1.0 = 15 pts. PEG = P/E ratio ÷ EPS growth rate. A PEG below 1.0 means you're paying less than the growth rate — good value. A PEG above 2.0 = 0 pts — you're overpaying for growth. Even a great business is a bad investment if you overpay for it.
Conviction Tiers — How Much to Invest
TIER-1
EMPIRE ≥ 70 AND PE ≤ 60
Allocation: 8–12% of portfolio per stock
Highest conviction. The business is exceptional AND reasonably priced. Maximum 5 Tier-1 stocks to avoid over-concentration. These are your "buy and hold forever" positions.
TIER-2
EMPIRE 50–69, OR EMPIRE ≥ 70 with PE 60–80
Allocation: 4–6% of portfolio per stock
Good quality but either the score is lower or the price is a bit rich. Still worth owning — just don't overweight. Maximum 8 Tier-2 positions.
TIER-3 / WATCHLIST
EMPIRE < 50, OR PE > 80 regardless of score
Allocation: 2–3% or watch only
Either the business quality is lower OR the price is speculative. A PE above 80 means you're paying 80 years of current earnings — even a 20% EPS miss triggers a 40–60% stock crash. No concentrated bets at these valuations.
💡
PE Cap Logic: Stocks with PE > 80 are automatically capped at Tier-3 regardless of how high their EMPIRE score is. Quality at 80× earnings is speculation — a single earnings disappointment can cut the stock in half. The system protects you from concentrating capital in high-quality businesses at bubble valuations.

Glossary of Key Terms
Quick reference for all trading terms used in this application
ATRAverage True Range — the average daily price move of a stock over 14 days. Used to set targets and stops in proportion to the stock's normal volatility.
EMAExponential Moving Average — like a simple average but more weight given to recent prices. 9 EMA reacts fast (intraday), 21 EMA is intermediate, 50 EMA is medium-term trend, 200 EMA is long-term trend.
PDH / PDLPrevious Day High / Previous Day Low — the price extremes from yesterday's session. Major support/resistance levels for intraday trading.
RSIRelative Strength Index — a momentum oscillator from 0–100. Above 70 = overbought (likely to pull back). Below 30 = oversold (likely to bounce). 40–65 is the sweet spot for new entries.
VIXVolatility Index — India VIX measures expected market volatility. Below 18 = calm. 18–22 = moderate. Above 22 = high fear. High VIX = reduce position sizes. Very high VIX (25+) = stay in cash.
VWAPVolume-Weighted Average Price — the average price weighted by volume traded. Institutional benchmark — they buy below VWAP and sell above. Price above VWAP = bullish session bias.
R (R-Multiple)A way to measure trade outcomes relative to risk. 1R = your defined risk (entry to stop-loss). A 2R winner means you made 2× your risk amount. A -1R loser means you lost exactly your planned risk.
Breakeven StopMoving your stop-loss to your entry price after reaching Target 1. You can no longer lose money on that trade. The worst outcome is a scratch (0R result). This is the most important trade management rule.
Expectancy(Win Rate × Avg Win R) + (Loss Rate × Avg Loss R). A positive expectancy means the system is profitable over many trades. 0.3R expectancy = you make 0.3× your risk on average per trade.
VCPVolatility Contraction Pattern — a Minervini pattern where a stock forms progressively tighter consolidations before breaking out. The "tightening coil" effect signals institutional accumulation.
ROEReturn on Equity — how much profit a business generates per rupee of shareholder capital. Above 20% = excellent. The single best measure of business quality.
PEG RatioPrice/Earnings-to-Growth ratio = P/E ÷ EPS growth rate. Below 1.0 = cheap relative to growth. Above 2.0 = expensive. The fairest single-number valuation metric for growth companies.
D/E RatioDebt-to-Equity — total debt divided by shareholder equity. Below 0.3 = low debt, financially strong. Above 0.5 = leveraged, risky in downturns.
Gap InvalidationA price level 1% beyond your entry trigger. If a stock opens above this level at market open (gap-up), the EOD setup is stale — the trigger was blown through overnight. Skip the trade.
Profit FactorTotal profit from winning trades ÷ total loss from losing trades. Above 1.5 = good. Above 2.0 = excellent. Below 1.0 = losing strategy.
Calmar RatioAnnual return ÷ maximum drawdown. Measures return per unit of pain. Above 1.0 = viable. Above 2.0 = strong. A system with 30% returns but 40% drawdown has poor Calmar (0.75).
